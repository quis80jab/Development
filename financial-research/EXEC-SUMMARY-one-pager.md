# EXEC SUMMARY — "Take Over" of a FL-Panhandle A&P/IA Shop (Oyster Aviation / KAAF?)

**Date:** 2026-05-30 · **For:** Arachnid Systems leadership · **Author:** Ben
**Decision:** Should Arachnid pursue an informal *operator-takeover* of a Florida-panhandle
aircraft-maintenance shop as a captive test-bed + certification on-ramp for ATLAS?

---

### THE OPPORTUNITY (corrected — it is NOT a sale)
A FL-panhandle A&P/IA owner posted on Reddit looking for **"an A&P IA to take over my shop —
I'm not selling it or hiring anyone, I just can't do it anymore."** On offer: a **5-plane
hangar on the field, 2 offices, conference room, partially-stocked parts room, all equipment
(except hand tools), low fixed costs, and planes already in the queue** (immediate revenue).
- Likely a **lease + equipment + customer handoff** — possibly little/no purchase price.
- **Binding requirement:** Arachnid must supply/hire a **certificated A&P/IA** to run it.
- **Unconfirmed:** post says only "Florida panhandle" — the Oyster Aviation / KAAF identity is
  *our inference*, not stated. (KAAF = Apalachicola Rgnl: county-owned, non-towered, three
  5,000+ ft runways, ~zero based aircraft — an ideal quiet flight-test field.)

### WHY IT COULD MATTER TO ARACHNID
ATLAS = autonomous **multi-sensor aircraft NDT** (6 modalities, Jetson Orin edge, ITAR; pilots
w/ Lockheed, GDIT, NASA KSC, AFRL, DIU). The shop is a strategic adjacency:
1. **Captive test & validation bed** — real aircraft + hangar to run ATLAS daily and build
   labeled defect datasets (today we borrow partner ramp time).
2. **Certification on-ramp** — in-house **A&P/IA authority** = a controlled path to getting
   *autonomous* inspection accepted into signed-off maintenance workflows.
3. **Quiet airfield** for ground-robotics + future UAS/sensor flight test.
4. **Ground-truth data flywheel** feeding the "predictive" ATLAS claim.

### MARKET CONTEXT (the tailwind is real)
- **A&P shortage is structural:** Boeing projects **710k** new techs needed/20 yrs; ~1/3 of
  AMT school seats unfilled. Owning scarce certificated labor *plus* automation sits on the
  chokepoint.
- **Robotic/drone NDT:** ~$1.24B (2025) → $2.52B (2030), **15% CAGR**, on a **$78B** MRO base.
- **Vertical integration is the proven M&A playbook** (AAR's $35M Aircraft Reconfig buy for
  cert capability; AxioAero, AE Industrial, Ronin, ST Engineering roll-ups) — but those comps
  are **$35M+ Part 145 stations**, not a $130/hr piston-GA shop. This shop is *strategically
  analogous, financially sub-scale.*
- **Competition bifurcating:** crowded, certification-gated commercial-visual lane (**Donecle**
  — Airbus/Boeing/EASA/FAA approved, €10M raise; **Mainblades**) vs. Arachnid's defensible
  **defense + multi-spectral/subsurface NDT + air-gapped edge** wedge (closest NDT peer:
  **Apellix**; incumbent **Lufthansa Technik** MORFI/CAIRE). *Protect the wedge; don't chase
  Donecle on commercial visual.*

### RISKS
- **Key-person:** value is the owner's labor + cert authority ("can't do it anymore"). Without
  a retained/embedded A&P/IA, the asset evaporates.
- **Mission drift:** a piston-GA wrench shop is a thin-margin services business far from
  defense-AI R&D — distraction risk vs. tiny financial weight.
- **Wrong fleet:** Cessna/Piper/Cirrus ≠ ATLAS's fighter/transport/widebody targets; piston-GA
  work only partially validates the real use cases.
- **ITAR/security mismatch:** open public field vs. our air-gapped/ITAR posture.
- **Leasehold, not land:** county/FAA grant-assurance rules; limited control.

### RECOMMENDATION
**Pursue only as a cheap *capability + test-bed* play, never as an MRO business buy.**
1. **Verify identity & terms** — DM the owner; confirm it's Oyster/KAAF, what conveys (lease,
   IA authority, equipment), and whether any money changes hands.
2. **Solve the A&P/IA first** — line up a certificated operator (hire/partner/retain the owner
   on earnout) *before* committing; it's the gating constraint.
3. **Frame the value test:** does a captive non-towered field + hangar + cert authority
   *materially accelerate ATLAS validation/certification* vs. today's partner-access model? If
   yes → low cost, high strategic ROI. If the work stays piston-GA and never bridges to defense
   platforms → walk away.
