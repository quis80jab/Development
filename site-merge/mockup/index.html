[01] Autonomous aerospace & defense inspection · validated 2026

Every Surface.
Every Defect.
Every Time.

Arachnid Systems is an autonomous robotics company building ATLAS, a multi-sensor inspection platform for aerospace, defense, energy, and space-launch assets. One ATLAS scan replaces three to four mechanics over six-plus hours of manual work with a 0.5 FTE, 18-to-25-minute autonomous pass at 98% defect-detection accuracy. The platform is validated on a Delta TechOps Boeing 737-800 at 98.3% accuracy in a 23-minute cycle, with FEAM Aero pilots underway in parallel. Customers do not buy a robot — they buy scans. Pricing replaces a $1,200-$1,800 manual narrowbody inspection with $163-$325 per autonomous scan, a 73-86% cost reduction at fleet scale.

ATLAS integrates six fused sensor modalities — hyperspectral imaging, LWIR thermal, LiDAR, laser profilometry, radar non-destructive testing, and polarimetric RGB — into a single autonomous pass. It sees what visual-only inspection cannot: corrosion under paint, composite delamination, subsurface fatigue, thermal disbonding. Outputs flow into AMOS, Trax, Rusada, and Quantum MRO systems as audit-ready records with a blockchain-verified Part 135 / 145 trail, and into ATLAS Connect — our predictive-maintenance service that forecasts component failures up to six months out using LSTM and XGBoost models trained on 16M+ flight hours of commercial aviation data.

Headquartered in Miami, Florida, with operations in Cincinnati, El Segundo, and Melbourne, Arachnid Systems was founded by five operators from Lockheed Martin, NASA, Apple, Tesla, USAF, Oracle, Nokia, and Epic Games. ATLAS is active at NASA Kennedy Space Center on an Artemis-tied pilot, under evaluation at AFRL for hyperspectral composite analysis, and at DIU for F-35 depot-level inspection, with a 20-aircraft lessor LOI signed for commercial fleet evaluation. The company is ITAR-compliant and on the seven-phase FAA Caprico approval pathway. Schedule a live demo to see exactly what ATLAS detects, and what manual inspection leaves behind.
